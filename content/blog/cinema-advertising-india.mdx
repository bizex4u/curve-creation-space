Cinema advertising in India occupies a unique position in the media mix — no other advertising environment matches its combination of high-quality visual display, captive audience and emotional engagement. India's cinema advertising market has recovered strongly since 2022 and is growing with multiplex expansion into Tier 2 cities.

This guide covers every cinema advertising format, multiplex chain costs and how to plan an effective cinema campaign.

## Why cinema advertising works

Cinema advertising has three structural advantages no other format matches:

**Visual impact**: A 70-foot cinema screen is the largest advertising canvas in any media format. Creative quality on screen dramatically outperforms the same creative on any other medium.

**Captive audience**: Cinema audiences are seated, engaged and in a receptive entertainment mindset. They cannot skip, scroll or switch channels. Pre-show slide advertising runs in a distraction-free environment.

**Emotional context**: Entertainment creates emotional receptivity. Audiences in an engaged, positive emotional state are more receptive to brand messaging than commuters, web browsers or TV viewers during ad breaks.

## Cinema advertising formats in India

### Pre-show Slide Advertising

Static image slides displayed on screen before the movie starts — typically 1–2 minutes of slides from multiple advertisers, running 15–30 minutes before the film.

**What it delivers**: Captive screen-facing audience before film starts. Typically 2–3 ads per advertiser in the pre-show rotation.

**Rate ranges** (per screen per week):
- Single-screen (A-class): ₹2,000–5,000
- Multiplex (PVR, INOX, Cinepolis): ₹5,000–15,000
- Premium multiplex (PVR Director's Cut, INOX Insignia): ₹15,000–35,000

### Pre-show Video Advertising (TVC Screening)

30-second to 60-second video spots screened in the pre-show period. Higher impact than slides. Multiple formats — can be placed at specific positions (last before film, first in sequence).

**Rate ranges** (per screen per week):
- Standard multiplex: ₹8,000–25,000
- Premium multiplex: ₹20,000–60,000
- Full national multiplex campaign (PVR network): ₹50 lakh–3 crore depending on screens and weeks

### In-Film Brand Integration

Your brand is integrated into the film itself — product placement, prop placement, dialogue integration or brand-logo placement in scene backgrounds. The highest-impact cinema advertising format.

**How it works**: Negotiated during production, not pre-release. BIZEX4U works with film production companies to identify integration opportunities for your brand category.

**Rate ranges**: ₹25 lakh–2 crore+ per film depending on integration level, film scale and lead placement.

### Foyer Activations

Physical brand activations in the multiplex foyer — sampling, product demonstration, contest entry, photo booths. Direct consumer interaction in a high-footfall, captive environment.

**Rate ranges**:
- Single multiplex foyer activation: ₹50,000–2 lakh per week
- City-wide multiplex foyer activation (5–10 properties): ₹3–12 lakh per week

### Concession Counter Branding

Branding at concession counters — popcorn tub advertising, counter signage and display panels near the F&B purchase point. High repeat exposure as most audience members visit concessions.

**Rate ranges**: Per screen per month: ₹5,000–20,000.

### Ticket Sleeve Advertising

Brand advertising printed on ticket sleeves or booking confirmations. Seen by every audience member who receives a physical ticket.

## India's multiplex cinema chains

### PVR Cinemas
India's largest multiplex chain — 850+ screens across 180+ cities. National reach. Premium and standard format options. Well-managed advertising sales function.

**National PVR campaign**: ₹80 lakh–5 crore for full pre-show video campaign depending on screens, cities and duration.

### INOX Leisure
India's second-largest multiplex operator — 650+ screens, strong in Maharashtra, MP, Rajasthan and growing nationally.

**Regional INOX campaign**: ₹30–80 lakh for 50-100 screen packages.

### Cinepolis India
International multiplex operator with strong premium footprint. Popular in Tier 2 cities.

### Miraj Cinemas
Regional multiplex chain — strong in North India, Rajasthan and Tier 2 cities. Good for regional campaign targeting.

### Carnival Cinemas
Largest single-screen cinema chain in India — 450+ properties. Tier 2 and Tier 3 coverage that multiplex chains don't reach.

### Regional single screens
India has 5,000+ single-screen cinemas. Despite reduced importance versus multiplexes, single screens remain the primary exhibition format in Tier 3 cities and smaller towns. Important for FMCG brands targeting rural and semi-urban markets.

## Cinema advertising costs: national vs regional campaigns

### National multiplex campaign (all chains, 100+ screens)
For a 30-second pre-show TVC campaign running for 4 weeks:
- **Economy (300–400 screens)**: ₹40–80 lakh
- **Standard (500–700 screens)**: ₹70–150 lakh
- **Premium (900+ screens, all chains)**: ₹1.5–3 crore

### Regional campaign (single city, select multiplexes)
- **Mumbai (10 multiplexes, 4 weeks)**: ₹8–20 lakh
- **Delhi (8 multiplexes, 4 weeks)**: ₹6–15 lakh
- **Bangalore (6 multiplexes, 4 weeks)**: ₹4–10 lakh

### Single-screen regional campaign (Tier 2/3 cities)
- **Per screen per month**: ₹3,000–8,000
- **Network of 50 single screens**: ₹1.5–4 lakh per month

## Cinema advertising and barter

Cinema advertising is partially barter-eligible:

**Regional cinema chains and single screens**: Most flexible for barter. Accept FMCG, food & beverage, personal care and consumer goods. Particularly common in Tier 2 cities.

**Carnival Cinemas**: Has established barter frameworks for FMCG and consumer goods.

**PVR and INOX**: Primarily cash-based advertising. Barter is considered case-by-case and typically requires a direct commercial agreement with the media sales team.

## When to use cinema advertising

Cinema advertising works best for:

**New product launches**: The premium, high-impact environment creates an association between your new product and high production value.

**Brand repositioning**: Cinema's emotional context helps shift brand perceptions — particularly useful for brands moving upmarket.

**Entertainment categories**: Movies, OTT platforms, gaming, streaming — natural fit with cinema audience's interests.

**Premium FMCG and lifestyle**: Popcorn brands, premium beverages, personal care — categories where the audience's leisure mindset aligns with the product.

**Geographic launch markets**: A city-by-city cinema roll-out aligns with retail launch sequences in new distribution markets.

Cinema advertising works less well for:

- Direct response objectives (no conversion mechanism)
- Mass-market utility brands (audience quality premium not justified)
- Very short campaigns (frequency needs minimum 3–4 weeks to build recall)
