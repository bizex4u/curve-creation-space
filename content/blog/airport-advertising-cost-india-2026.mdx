Airport advertising in India has seen consistent rate increases since 2022 — driven by expanding digital inventory, rising passenger volumes and the premium status of airports as a brand environment. This guide covers realistic 2026 rate ranges for every major Indian airport format, drawn from recent campaign executions.

## Why airport advertising rates matter more now

The Indian aviation market crossed 150 million domestic passengers in FY2025 and is on track for 200 million by 2028. More passengers means more advertising demand — and rates have reflected this. But understanding what you actually get for those rates is what separates effective airport campaigns from expensive ones.

## Rate structure: how airport advertising is priced

Airport advertising in India is priced on three dimensions:

- **Format**: Static lightbox, large-format static, digital screen (DOOH), aerobridge vinyl, floor graphic
- **Location**: Terminal, zone within terminal (landside/airside/arrivals), specific corridor
- **Duration**: 30-day minimum, with discounts for 60–90 day bookings

Most airport media in India is controlled by a small number of concessionaires — Times OOH, Adsyndicate, Bright Outdoor — who hold long-term CONCESSION agreements with AAI and private airport operators. Rates are negotiated, not listed.

## Delhi IGI Airport advertising rates 2026

Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport is India's busiest — and most expensive — airport advertising environment.

### T3 International Departures
The most premium zone at IGI. International departures passengers are high-income, well-travelled and spend 90+ minutes in the terminal.

- **Large-format static (8×4 ft lightbox)**: ₹8–18 lakh per month
- **DOOH screen (55–75 inch)**: ₹3–8 lakh per month
- **Mega-format static (20+ ft)**: ₹25–60 lakh per month

### T3 Domestic Departures
Similar audience quality to international but higher volume.

- **Platform panels**: ₹4–10 lakh per month
- **Concourse DOOH**: ₹2–6 lakh per month
- **Gate lounge panels**: ₹3–8 lakh per month

### Aerobridges at IGI
The most exclusive and highest-impact format at Delhi airport. Each aerobridge vinyl covers the entire boarding ramp — seen by every passenger on that flight.

- **Single aerobridge**: ₹5–15 lakh per month
- **Multiple aerobridges package**: ₹25–60 lakh per month

### Baggage Claim — IGI
Overhead and wall formats at T1, T2 and T3 baggage belts. Passengers wait 15–25 minutes here — among the highest dwell times at any airport touchpoint.

- **Overhead banners**: ₹3–8 lakh per month
- **Wall lightboxes**: ₹2–5 lakh per month

## Barter airport advertising: can you avoid cash?

Yes — airport advertising is partially barter-eligible. A subset of formats at most Indian airports can be purchased using inventory exchange rather than cash. Common barter-eligible categories include:

- FMCG and packaged goods
- Consumer electronics
- Hospitality and hotel inventory
- Food & beverage products
- Lifestyle and fashion accessories

Not all formats are barter-eligible — premium T3 and T2 formats at major airports typically require cash or hybrid deals. Barter ratios for airport advertising typically range from 1:1 to 1:1.5.

## How to get the best airport advertising rates

Several factors influence the rates you actually pay versus published rate cards:

**1. Campaign duration**: 60–90 day campaigns typically attract 10–20% discounts vs 30-day rates. Long-term partnerships (6–12 months) can secure 25–35% off rate card.

**2. Multi-airport packages**: Booking campaigns across 3+ airports in one plan often gets better bundle rates from concessionaires who cover multiple airports.

**3. Off-peak timing**: Q1 (January–March) tends to be slightly softer in demand. Campaigns launched in this window often get better positions at better rates.

**4. Barter component**: Hybrid deals with a barter component often unlock additional inventory that pure-cash buyers can't access.

**5. Agency relationships**: Agencies with established relationships at CSIA, IGI and BLR concessionaires have access to non-listed inventory and preferred pricing.

## What do airport advertising campaigns actually deliver?

The metrics that matter for airport advertising:

- **DEC (Daily Effective Circulation)**: Published by media owners based on footfall data and panel exposure. Typically 40–70% of terminal passenger count for well-positioned formats.
- **Reach**: Monthly unique passenger count exposed to your format.
- **Frequency**: Average number of times a unique passenger sees your ad in a month (relevant for recurring travellers).
- **CPM**: Cost per thousand impressions — airport advertising CPMs range ₹150–800 depending on format and airport, with airside premium formats at the higher end.
