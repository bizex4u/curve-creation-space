Delhi Metro is India's most extensive urban transit network — 390+ stations, 286 km, 12 lines, 60 lakh daily passengers. For brands targeting urban Delhi's working population, DMRC advertising offers unmatched frequency at a cost structure that out-performs most comparable urban media.

This guide covers realistic 2026 rate ranges for every DMRC advertising format, how rates vary by line and location, and how to plan a campaign that actually builds recall.

## DMRC advertising: who manages it?

Delhi Metro Rail Corporation does not sell advertising directly. DMRC advertising rights are held by appointed concessionaires — primarily Laqshya Media and Vishwapriya Group — through long-term contracts. Rates are negotiated with these concessionaires, not DMRC directly.

This means published rate cards are starting points. Experienced agencies negotiate significantly better positions and rates than what's listed. Volume, duration and campaign timing all affect final rates.

## Delhi Metro advertising formats and 2026 rates

### Platform Panels (Backlit Lightboxes)

Platform panels are the backbone of DMRC advertising — backlit lightboxes on platforms across every station on the network. These are seen by waiting and boarding passengers at close range.

**Rate ranges 2026:**
- Single station, single panel: ₹15,000–40,000 per month
- Line-wide campaign (all stations on one line): ₹2.5–6 lakh per month
- Yellow Line (highest volume): ₹3.5–8 lakh per month
- Blue Line (Dwarka–Vaishali/Noida): ₹3–7 lakh per month
- Network-wide (all lines, all stations): ₹25–45 lakh per month

**Best for**: Brand awareness, new product launches, frequency-dependent campaigns (FMCG, apps, finance).

### Train Exterior Wraps

Full train body wraps — your creative covers the entire exterior of a DMRC train set. The wrapped train runs its scheduled route, exposing your brand at every station it stops at across the day.

**Rate ranges 2026:**
- Single train wrap (30 days): ₹8–18 lakh per month
- Premium route (Yellow Line, Blue Line): ₹12–22 lakh per month
- Multi-train package: Negotiated

**Best for**: High-impact launches, brand repositioning, category dominance statements.

### Station Dominations

A station domination means branding every available ad format within a single station — platforms, concourse, fare gates and corridors — under a unified campaign creative.

**Key stations and 2026 rate ranges:**
- Rajiv Chowk (India's busiest interchange): ₹20–45 lakh per month
- Kashmere Gate (5-line interchange): ₹12–25 lakh per month
- Hauz Khas (Yellow–Magenta interchange): ₹8–18 lakh per month
- Botanical Garden (Blue–Aqua interchange): ₹6–14 lakh per month
- Central Secretariat (Yellow–Violet interchange): ₹8–18 lakh per month

**Best for**: Product launches, brand events, seasonal campaign peaks, category ownership at high-traffic hubs.

### Concourse Displays

Large-format lightboxes and DOOH screens in station concourses — the entry/exit zone seen by 100% of passengers entering and leaving the station.

**Rate ranges 2026:**
- Standard concourse panel: ₹20,000–60,000 per month per station
- Major interchange concourse: ₹80,000–2 lakh per month

### Train Interior Panels

Adhesive panels inside metro coaches — seen by seated and standing passengers for the full journey duration. Underground sections have no phone signal, making interior panels even more impactful.

**Rate ranges 2026:**
- Per coach, per month: ₹3,000–8,000
- Full train interior (all coaches): ₹40,000–1.2 lakh per month
- Interior campaign across 10 trains: ₹4–12 lakh per month

**Best for**: Direct response, QR-code campaigns, app downloads, financial services, insurance.

### Fare Gate Branding

Vinyl wrapping on fare gate turnstiles — seen at close range by 100% of entering and exiting passengers.

**Rate ranges 2026:**
- Per station, fare gate set: ₹30,000–80,000 per month
- Network-wide fare gate campaign: Custom pricing

## Which Delhi Metro lines perform best for advertising?

### Yellow Line (Samaypur Badli–Huda City Centre)
India's most commercially attractive metro line — runs through Central Delhi, CP, Hauz Khas, Saket, IFFCO Chowk and Gurgaon. Passenger profile: young professionals, retail shoppers, CBD commuters.

**Best for**: FMCG, apparel, food delivery, financial services, premium retail.

### Blue Line (Dwarka–Vaishali and Dwarka–Noida)
Delhi's longest metro line — covers Dwarka, Central Delhi (Rajiv Chowk, Barakhamba), Noida and Vaishali. High-volume but more mass-market audience mix.

**Best for**: Consumer goods, FMCG, education, financial inclusion products.

### Magenta Line (Janakpuri West–Botanical Garden)
Connects South Delhi to Noida. Passengers skew slightly more affluent — South Delhi residential zones (Hauz Khas, Greater Kailash, Okhla).

**Best for**: Lifestyle, D2C, premium FMCG, healthcare.

### Pink Line (Majlis Park–Shiv Vihar)
Delhi's newest major line — covers North Delhi residential zones and Anand Vihar interchange.

**Best for**: Consumer durables, education, mass-market FMCG.

## How to calculate DMRC campaign ROI

DMRC advertising delivers frequency-heavy reach — the same commuter sees your brand 20–22 times per month on a line campaign. For recall-based categories (FMCG, apps, finance), this frequency is extremely valuable.

**Rough CPM benchmarks for DMRC advertising 2026:**
- Platform panels (line campaign): ₹8–18 per thousand impressions
- Station domination (top interchange): ₹25–50 per thousand impressions
- Interior panels: ₹12–25 per thousand impressions

These compare favourably to digital performance advertising CPMs (₹80–200) for the same demographic.

## Barter advertising on Delhi Metro

DMRC advertising is barter-eligible for select formats through approved concessionaires. Categories accepted include:

- FMCG and packaged consumer goods
- Beverages (non-alcoholic)
- Consumer electronics and accessories
- Lifestyle and fashion products
- Food & beverage brands

Barter ratio for DMRC advertising: typically 1:1 to 1:1.2. A hybrid deal (partial cash, partial barter) is the most common structure for barter-eligible categories.
