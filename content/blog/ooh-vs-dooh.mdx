OOH (Out-of-Home) and DOOH (Digital Out-of-Home) are often treated as interchangeable. They're not. Each has distinct cost structures, creative constraints, targeting capabilities and campaign contexts where it outperforms the other. Understanding the difference is the starting point for any effective outdoor campaign.

## What is OOH advertising?

Traditional OOH covers all static outdoor formats — printed vinyl, flex or paper mounted on physical structures. In India this includes:

- **Hoardings and billboards**: Mounted on steel structures along roads and highways
- **Unipoles**: Single-pole structures, typically 20–40 ft height, roadside
- **Gantries**: Structures spanning multiple lanes across highways — highest-impact format
- **Transit formats**: Bus shelters, railway station panels, metro platform lightboxes
- **Mall and retail**: Entry canopies, atrium banners, walkway panels

OOH is booked for fixed periods — typically 30, 60 or 90 days. The creative is printed and installed at campaign start; changes require reprinting and physical reinstallation.

## What is DOOH advertising?

DOOH replaces printed vinyl with digital screens — LED or LCD displays connected to a content management system. Campaigns run as scheduled slots on digital screens.

Key DOOH formats in India:

- **Roadside LED hoardings**: Large LED screens on highway structures — same locations as traditional hoardings but digital
- **Mall screens**: In-mall DOOH networks in malls and shopping centres
- **Metro and transit DOOH**: Digital screens at metro stations, airports and bus terminals
- **Cinema lobby DOOH**: Digital screens in multiplex foyers
- **Petrol pump DOOH**: Screens at fuel stations — high repeat exposure

## The 7 key differences: OOH vs DOOH

### 1. Creative flexibility

**OOH**: One creative per booking period. Printing takes 3–5 days. Changes cost ₹15,000–50,000 in print and labour per site. If your campaign creative becomes outdated (a date mention, a sold-out product), you're stuck with it.

**DOOH**: Creative changes in minutes. Update remotely across any number of screens simultaneously. Run A/B variants. React to news, weather, competitor activity or brand moments in real time. Show different creative at different times of day.

**Winner**: DOOH. Creative flexibility is its defining advantage.

### 2. Cost

**OOH**: Lower base cost per site. A static hoarding at a prime Gurgaon location might cost ₹2–5 lakh per month. No slot-sharing — your brand owns 100% of the format for the entire booking period.

**DOOH**: Higher CPM, but your ad is shown in rotation with other brands (typically 8–12 slots, 10 seconds each on a 90–120 second loop). For the same visual impact, DOOH at equivalent locations costs 30–60% more than static OOH. However, creative production costs are lower.

**Winner**: OOH for sustained presence. DOOH for campaign flexibility and dynamic creative.

### 3. Targeting and dayparting

**OOH**: Zero targeting beyond geography. You choose the site, you get everyone who passes it 24/7. No dayparting, no audience segment selection.

**DOOH**: Supports dayparting — show different creative at different times. Target morning commuters with a coffee brand at 7–10 AM, lunch audiences with a food delivery app at noon, and evening commuters with an OTT platform at 6–9 PM, all on the same screen.

**Winner**: DOOH, decisively. Dayparting alone can double campaign relevance.

### 4. Measurement

**OOH**: Measured by Daily Effective Circulation (DEC) — estimated reach based on traffic counts and research. No real-time data. Post-campaign measurement relies on brand studies or sales correlation analysis.

**DOOH**: Increasingly connected to real-time audience data — traffic cameras, mobile anonymised footfall data and audience intelligence platforms provide impression tracking. Some DOOH networks offer programmatic buying with impression guarantees.

**Winner**: DOOH. Measurement is improving rapidly and already significantly better than static OOH.

### 5. Brand presence — share of voice

**OOH**: 100% share of voice on your format for 100% of the time. Your brand is the only brand on that billboard for the entire month. This exclusivity is OOH's strongest differentiator.

**DOOH**: Your ad runs in rotation with competing brands — typically 8–12 ads per screen loop. You own 8–12% of screen time, not 100%. If a competitor buys the same screen, your share of voice drops.

**Winner**: OOH. Exclusivity of static formats is a major advantage for brand dominance campaigns.

### 6. Installation and lead time

**OOH**: Print and installation typically takes 5–10 days from creative sign-off. Logistics intensive, especially for multi-city campaigns.

**DOOH**: Campaigns go live in 24–48 hours after creative approval. Multi-city launches can activate simultaneously. No installation cost, no logistics.

**Winner**: DOOH. Especially valuable for time-sensitive campaigns.

### 7. Barter eligibility

**OOH**: Widely barter-eligible across India. Static OOH site owners readily accept inventory in exchange for advertising space — the most mature barter advertising format in India.

**DOOH**: Less commonly barter-eligible. Digital screen networks prefer cash transactions due to their operating costs. Exceptions exist, but the OOH barter market is significantly larger.

**Winner**: OOH for barter campaigns.

## When to choose OOH

Choose static OOH when:

- **You need 24/7 brand presence**: Launches, repositioning, building long-term brand awareness
- **Single creative, long duration**: Your campaign message won't change for 60–90 days
- **Budget efficiency matters**: You want lowest cost per hour of exposure
- **Barter campaign**: You're exchanging inventory for advertising
- **Premium location ownership**: You want to own a landmark site exclusively

Best categories for OOH-led campaigns: FMCG, real estate, automotive, education, telecom, mass retail.

## When to choose DOOH

Choose DOOH when:

- **Creative needs to change**: Sale events, date-specific offers, countdown clocks
- **Dayparting adds value**: Your audience has peak relevance at specific times of day
- **Speed matters**: Campaign needs to launch in days, not weeks
- **Contextual relevance**: Weather triggers, live sports scores, real-time promotions
- **Programmatic buying**: You want impression-based buying and measurement

Best categories for DOOH-led campaigns: QSR, retail sales, delivery apps, OTT platforms, finance, luxury lifestyle.

| Format | Channel | Spend |
|--------|---------|-------|
| 3 OOH hoardings, Ring Road | Static | ₹8–15 lakh |
| 5 DOOH screens, Gurgaon malls | Digital | ₹4–10 lakh |
| Combined campaign | OOH + DOOH | ₹12–25 lakh |

The combined campaign delivers more touchpoints, better targeting and higher creative impact than either format alone.
