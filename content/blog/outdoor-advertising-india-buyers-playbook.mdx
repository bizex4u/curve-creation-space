Outdoor advertising in India is a ₹4,500+ crore market — fragmented, relationship-driven and frequently mispriced. This playbook covers everything a media buyer or brand marketer needs to know to get the most from outdoor in 2026.

## The Indian outdoor market: what you're actually buying

OOH in India is not a commodity. The same site type in different locations has dramatically different value. A 40×20 ft hoarding on NH48 in Gurgaon is worth 5–10x the same size hoarding on a secondary road in the same city.

What you're buying in outdoor advertising:

**Location**: The single most important variable. Traffic count, audience profile, visibility angle, competition from other hoardings, whether the site is on the left or right side of the road (relative to traffic flow).

**Format**: Static flex, static vinyl, backlit lightbox, LED/DOOH. Each has different creative requirements, visual impact and cost structure.

**Exclusivity**: You own 100% of the format for the booking period (static). DOOH shares impressions with other advertisers.

**Permit status**: All outdoor advertising in India requires municipal permits. Unpermitted sites are regularly removed — a campaign lost mid-flight.

## Outdoor advertising formats in India

### Hoardings (Standard Billboards)
The most common OOH format. Steel structures with printed flex or vinyl. Sizes range from 10×5 ft to 100×40 ft — the most common commercial sizes are 20×10, 30×15 and 40×20 ft.

- **Backlit hoardings**: Internally illuminated, visible at night. Higher impact, higher cost.
- **Frontlit hoardings**: Externally lit by flood lamps. Common in markets where backlit structures aren't viable.
- **Unlit hoardings**: No artificial illumination. Cheapest option — limited to high-ambient-light locations (major daytime roads).

### Unipoles
Single-pole structures, typically 20–40 ft tall, allowing 360° visibility. More premium than standard hoardings due to height advantage and visibility in multiple directions. Common on highway medians and roundabouts.

### Gantries
Structures spanning across multiple road lanes — the most impactful OOH format in India. Unavoidable: drivers must pass under them. Available on major national highways and expressways. Very limited supply, premium pricing.

### Kiosks and Street Furniture
Advertising panels on bus shelters, public kiosks and street furniture. Government-controlled in most Indian cities — MCGM (Mumbai), NDMC (Delhi), BBMP (Bangalore). More process-intensive to book but delivers strong pedestrian-level reach.

### Flex Banners and Wall Murals
Lower-cost options — flex printed banners on walls, compound walls and temporary structures. Very cost-efficient for Tier 2 and Tier 3 city campaigns. Short booking periods (7–15 days).

## City-by-city OOH market guide

### Delhi
India's largest OOH market — fragmented among NDMC, MCD, PWD and private site owners. Ring Road and flyover hoardings command the highest rates. Political advertising history means many prime sites have restrictions.

**Key corridors**: NH48 (toward Gurgaon), Ring Road network, ITO flyover, NH1 (toward Punjab), NH8
**Rate range for prime corridor**: ₹2–20 lakh per month
**Permit authority**: NDMC (Central Delhi), MCD (rest of Delhi), PWD (state highways)

### Mumbai
Mumbai's outdoor market is concentrated along the Western Expressway (WEH) and Eastern Expressway (EEH). Hoardings require MCGM permits — a process that can take 4–8 weeks.

**Key corridors**: WEH (Bandra–Borivali), Eastern Expressway, BKC, Lower Parel–Worli corridor
**Rate range for prime corridor**: ₹3–25 lakh per month
**Permit authority**: MCGM

### Bangalore
Bangalore OOH is concentrated on ORR and major arterial roads. BBMP permit process is relatively streamlined.

**Key corridors**: Outer Ring Road, Old Airport Road, MG Road, Indiranagar
**Rate range for prime corridor**: ₹1.5–12 lakh per month
**Permit authority**: BBMP

### Hyderabad
Hyderabad has strong OOH inventory on its ORR and HITECH City corridor — lower competition and rates than Mumbai or Delhi for equivalent locations.

**Key corridors**: Outer Ring Road, HITECH City Road, Jubilee Hills, Banjara Hills
**Rate range for prime corridor**: ₹1–8 lakh per month
**Permit authority**: GHMC

### Pune
Growing OOH market — software industry in Hinjewadi and residential expansion make Pune an increasingly attractive mid-market OOH buy.

**Key corridors**: Pune-Mumbai Expressway, Hinjewadi, Koregaon Park, Baner Road
**Rate range**: ₹60,000–5 lakh per month

## Site selection criteria for outdoor advertising

### The SCORE framework

**S — Size and visibility**: Is the format large enough to create impact from the viewing distance? Is it visible without obstruction?

**C — Count (traffic)**: What is the verified daily traffic count? Ask for audited DEC data, not operator estimates.

**O — Orientation**: Is the site on the left or right side of traffic flow? Left-hand sites are generally preferred (driver's eye line). Corner sites visible from two directions are premium.

**R — Relevance**: Does the audience profile of the location match your target demographic? A premium hoarding in the right catchment is worth more than a larger hoarding in the wrong geography.

**E — Exclusivity**: Is this a dominant site or one of many in a cluster? A single large hoarding on an otherwise clean road is worth significantly more than one of five hoardings at the same intersection.

## Barter outdoor advertising

Static OOH is India's most barter-friendly advertising channel. The market structure — thousands of independent site owners, each looking for ways to reduce client defaults and manage payments — makes barter natural.

### How barter OOH works

An OOH site owner accepts products from the advertiser in lieu of monthly site rental. They use these products internally (staff welfare, canteen), sell them to staff at a discount, or use them for client gifting.

The formal barter structure:
1. Site owner agrees to accept product at a defined rate
2. BIZEX4U issues a purchase order from brand to site owner (for advertising)
3. Site owner issues a counter PO to the brand (for product)
4. Advertising runs; product is delivered on schedule

All barter transactions are GST-compliant — both POs carry applicable GST, which both parties offset against their tax liabilities.

### Which OOH operators accept barter?

Most independent OOH operators (smaller regional players) readily accept barter. Large national operators (Times OOH, Laqshya, Clear Channel India) have formal barter policies and will consider barter for specific categories.

## Planning your 2026 outdoor campaign

### Step 1: Define the campaign objective

Different objectives require different site selection:

- **Brand awareness launch**: Maximum reach sites — highways, expressways, city entry points
- **Geographic expansion**: Sites in new distribution markets — city-specific, near retail outlets
- **Purchase intent**: Sites near retail — mall approaches, high street proximity
- **Competitive response**: Occupying dominant sites in markets where a competitor has recently activated
