Transit advertising reaches India's commuting population at the moment of maximum urban engagement — in motion, in public space, surrounded by the city. It's the advertising format that urban India spends the most time with each day, yet many brands still under-invest in it.

This guide covers every transit advertising format available in India, what each costs and which brands benefit most from each channel.

## Why transit advertising works in India

India's urban transit system carries hundreds of millions of passengers daily. Delhi Metro alone — 60 lakh daily passengers. Mumbai's suburban rail network — 75 lakh. Bus systems across 50 cities carry another 100 million+ combined.

Three structural factors make transit advertising effective:

**Captive audience**: Commuters on metro, buses and trains have limited alternatives to looking at the environment around them. Screen time is reduced in crowded transit. Brand exposure is relatively undistracted.

**Daily frequency**: The same commuter travels the same route 5 days a week — creating 20+ exposures per month to your campaign on a line-level booking. This frequency builds recall in a way that one-time high-reach media cannot replicate.

**Urban aspirational demographic**: Indian transit ridership skews 22–45, urban, employed and aspirational — the core target for FMCG, D2C, financial services, lifestyle and digital brands.

## Metro advertising in India

Metro advertising is the premium tier of Indian transit advertising — modern infrastructure, well-maintained environments and a quality audience association that bus advertising doesn't carry.

### Delhi Metro (DMRC)
India's largest metro network: 390+ stations, 286 km, 12 lines, 60 lakh daily passengers.

**Available formats**:
- Platform panels (backlit lightboxes)
- Station dominations
- Train exterior wraps
- Train interior panels
- Fare gate branding
- Concourse displays and DOOH

**Rate ranges**: Platform panels from ₹3–8 lakh per month per line. Station dominations at major interchanges: ₹12–45 lakh per month. Full network: ₹35–60 lakh per month.

### Mumbai Metro
Three operational lines (Line 1, 2A, 7) covering western and central Mumbai corridors. 5 lakh+ daily passengers, growing rapidly.

**Available formats**: Platform panels, train branding, concourse displays, interior panels.

**Rate ranges**: Single-line campaigns from ₹2–5 lakh per month.

→ [Mumbai Metro advertising guide](/mumbai-metro-advertising)

### Bangalore Metro (Namma Metro)
Green and Purple lines covering central and eastern Bangalore. 4 lakh+ daily passengers, strong tech professional audience.

**Rate ranges**: Line campaigns from ₹1.5–4 lakh per month.

### Hyderabad Metro (HMRL)
Three lines covering Hyderabad's major commercial corridors — HITECH City, Ameerpet, LB Nagar. Well-maintained, growing ridership.

**Rate ranges**: Line campaigns from ₹1–3 lakh per month.

### Chennai Metro
Two lines currently operational. Expanding significantly with new corridors under construction.

**Rate ranges**: Early mover advantage — lower rates, growing network.

### Kochi Metro
Premium compact network — strong tourist and local professional audience.

## Bus advertising in India

Bus advertising delivers the broadest reach of any transit format — buses reach areas and demographics that metro doesn't cover, including Tier 2 cities, suburban zones and lower-income urban neighbourhoods.

### BEST (Mumbai) bus advertising
Mumbai's BEST bus network carries 25 lakh+ daily passengers across 500+ routes. Formats include:

- **Exterior wraps**: Full bus body branding — seen across Mumbai's entire bus network
- **Interior panels**: Inside bus displays — seen by all seated passengers for journey duration
- **Bus shelter advertising**: Static and backlit panels at BEST bus stops across the city

**Rate ranges**: Full bus exterior wrap: ₹25,000–60,000 per bus per month. Interior panels: ₹5,000–15,000 per bus per month.

### DTC (Delhi) bus advertising
Delhi Transport Corporation buses — available for exterior and interior advertising.

### KSRTC/BMTC (Bangalore) bus advertising
Bangalore's bus network complements metro — covering areas between metro stations and suburban zones.

### City-specific bus advertising
Most major Indian cities have government bus advertising concessions — PMPML (Pune), APSRTC (Andhra), TSRTC (Telangana), UPSRTC (UP). Regional agencies manage bookings.

## Railway station advertising

Indian Railways carries 23 million passengers daily — the world's largest single-railway passenger system. Railway station advertising covers:

### Major station advertising
Premium large-format advertising at India's busiest railway stations:

- **Mumbai Central, Dadar, CST (CSMT)**: 10–15 lakh+ daily footfall. Large-format hoardings, static panels, DOOH.
- **Delhi Railway Stations (New Delhi, Hazrat Nizamuddin, Old Delhi)**: 5–8 lakh daily footfall.
- **Bangalore Yeshwanthpur, KSR Bangalore City**: 3–5 lakh daily footfall.

Railway station advertising is managed through Indian Railway Stations Development Corporation (IRSDC) and station-specific concessionaires.

**Rate ranges**: Prime station lightboxes: ₹50,000–2 lakh per month. DOOH screens at major stations: ₹1–4 lakh per month.

### Train compartment advertising
Interior panels inside train coaches — accessible during journey for the full travel duration. Long-distance train panels reach passengers for 4–24 hours. Rajdhani and Shatabdi trains carry a higher-income audience.

**Rate ranges**: Per compartment per month: ₹5,000–20,000. Full train interior (all compartments): ₹1–4 lakh per month.

## Auto-rickshaw and cab advertising

### Auto-rickshaw advertising
Auto-rickshaws are ubiquitous in Indian cities — rolling through traffic at slow speeds with high pedestrian visibility. Formats include:

- Rear panel advertising (most common)
- Hood wrap (full front body branding)
- Seat back panels (seen by passengers)

Auto-rickshaw advertising is extremely cost-efficient for hyperlocal campaigns — branding in specific markets, neighbourhoods or districts. Particularly effective in cities with high auto density (Mumbai, Pune, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore).

**Rate ranges**: Per auto per month: ₹800–2,500. Fleet of 100 autos: ₹80,000–2.5 lakh per month.

### Cab advertising (Ola, Uber, etc.)
App-based cab advertising covers headrest displays, in-cab tablet advertising and exterior branding. OLA Media and similar platforms allow geo-targeted cab advertising in specific cities and neighbourhoods.

**Rate ranges**: In-cab digital advertising (Ola Media): ₹50–200 CPM. Exterior branding: Available through specialist cab fleet owners.

## Planning a transit advertising campaign

### Define your transit mix by city

Not every transit channel is available in every city. For a national transit campaign:

| City | Metro | Bus | Railway |
|------|-------|-----|---------|
| Delhi | ✓ DMRC (best) | ✓ DTC | ✓ Multiple stations |
| Mumbai | ✓ Lines 1/2A/7 | ✓ BEST | ✓ CST, Dadar |
| Bangalore | ✓ Namma Metro | ✓ BMTC | ✓ KSR |
| Hyderabad | ✓ HMRL | ✓ TSRTC | ✓ Secunderabad |
| Chennai | ✓ CMRL | ✓ MTC | ✓ Chennai Central |

### Budget allocation principles

**Metro-heavy strategy (65–70% metro)**: Best for premium brands targeting urban aspirational audience at high frequency. Highest CPQ (Cost per Quality impression).

**Bus-heavy strategy (50–60% bus)**: Best for FMCG, mass-market consumer brands targeting broad urban reach at low CPM.

**Mixed transit strategy**: Combine metro (premium urban), bus (broad urban) and railway (inter-city, suburban) for maximum reach coverage.

### Barter for transit advertising

Transit advertising is barter-eligible through several channels:

- **Metro concessionaires**: DMRC and Mumbai Metro advertising concessionaires accept barter for select formats and product categories
- **Bus shelter advertising**: City-based shelter advertising operators often accept FMCG barter
- **Auto fleet owners**: Individual auto fleet operators readily accept FMCG in exchange for vehicle branding
