Mumbai's Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (CSIA) is India's second busiest airport by passenger volume — 50 million+ passengers annually — and arguably India's most commercially valuable airport advertising environment. It handles the highest concentration of high-net-worth individuals, international business travellers and premium leisure passengers of any Indian airport.

This guide covers everything brands need to know about advertising at Mumbai Airport: terminals, formats, rates and campaign strategy.

## Mumbai Airport: the advertising case

Mumbai's airport audience is structurally different from Delhi's:

**Higher international traffic share**: Mumbai handles a larger proportion of international passengers relative to total traffic than Delhi's IGI. International terminals reach travellers with higher spend and premium product affinity.

**Financial capital audience**: Mumbai is India's financial capital. Its airport's domestic terminals carry a disproportionate number of financial services professionals, corporate decision-makers and senior executives.

**Entertainment and media industry**: Bollywood and India's media industry are headquartered in Mumbai. Airport advertising here reaches creative directors, producers and entertainment executives — a uniquely influential audience for brand culture.

**Outbound travel market**: Mumbai has one of India's highest per-capita outbound tourism rates. Airport advertising reaches travellers with strong international brand exposure and aspirational lifestyle preferences.

## Mumbai Airport terminals

### T2 (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Terminal)
Mumbai Airport's main international terminal — one of the most architecturally significant airports in Asia. Art-forward design, premium retail environment and superior advertising inventory.

T2 handles:
- All international departures and arrivals
- Domestic operations for Air India, Vistara, IndiGo (most routes)
- Combined domestic + international terminal format

T2 advertising inventory includes the full premium range: large-format aerobridge vinyls, departure hall DOOH, security hold screens, gate lounge panels, baggage reclaim displays and international arrivals hall branding.

### T1 (Santacruz Domestic Terminal)
T1 handles domestic operations for SpiceJet, GoFirst and some IndiGo routes. Smaller scale, lower audience quality versus T2, but meaningful for mass-market brands targeting domestic travellers.

**T1 audience profile**: Broader domestic traveller profile — includes leisure travellers, VFR (visiting friends and relatives) passengers and price-sensitive travellers. Less premium than T2 but significantly cheaper CPM.

## Mumbai Airport advertising formats

### Aerobridge Vinyls
Full-surface vinyl wraps on the aerobridge connecting aircraft to terminal at T2. Every departing and arriving passenger on that route is exposed at 1–2 metre proximity during boarding and deboarding.

**Availability**: Limited — only available on specific aerobridges. Premium pricing reflects exclusivity and proximity.

**Rate ranges**: ₹3–8 lakh per aerobridge per month. International aerobridge routes command premium rates.

### Departure Hall Displays
Large-format static and DOOH panels in T2's departure hall — seen by 100% of departing passengers during check-in, security queue and departure floor movement. Longest dwell-time zone in the airport.

**Rate ranges**: Static panels from ₹80,000–2.5 lakh per month. Large-format DOOH: ₹2–8 lakh per month.

### Security Hold Advertising
Advertising panels in the security hold area — mandatory waiting zone where all passengers queue and are processed. Captive audience with 15–30 minute dwell time.

**Rate ranges**: Security hold panels: ₹1–4 lakh per month.

### Gate Lounge DOOH
Digital screens in gate lounges — one of the highest-engagement airport formats because gate lounge passengers are seated, waiting and in close proximity to screens for 20–60 minutes.

**Available formats**: Full-screen 10-second slots in rotation. 15 and 30-second spots also available for higher-impact messages.

**Rate ranges**: Gate lounge screen: ₹1.5–5 lakh per month per cluster.

### International Arrivals Hall
The international arrivals hall is Mumbai Airport's most premium advertising location — greeting incoming international passengers and the people meeting them. High HNW density in both groups.

**Rate ranges**: Arrivals hall formats from ₹3–12 lakh per month.

### Baggage Reclaim Hall
Baggage reclaim at T2 has mandatory 15–25 minute dwell time for all arriving passengers. Exposed to both domestic and international arriving passengers.

**Rate ranges**: Baggage carousel panels: ₹1.5–6 lakh per month.

### Car Park and Landside
Entry ramps, elevated walkways, car park displays and pre-departure landside areas. Lower dwell time but very high unique reach (all passengers must traverse landside before entering terminal).

**Rate ranges**: From ₹60,000–2 lakh per month.

### Transit Zones
T2's integrated domestic-international structure creates unique transit advertising opportunities — passengers connecting between domestic and international routes pass through dedicated transit lounges.

## Mumbai Airport vs Delhi Airport: which to choose?

Both airports are excellent advertising environments. The choice depends on your brand's targeting objectives:

| Factor | Mumbai (CSIA) | Delhi (IGI) |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Annual passengers | 50M+ | 70M+ |
| International mix | Higher % | Lower % |
| Business traveller profile | Financial services, media | Government, corporate, FMCG |
| Premium CPM | Higher | Lower |
| West India catchment | Strong | Weak |
| South India gateway | Partial | Weak |

**Choose Mumbai if**: Your core audience is financial sector, premium consumer, HNW households or international business.

**Choose Delhi if**: Your campaign prioritises scale, national coverage or northern/central India business reach.

**Choose both**: For premium national brand campaigns — a Mumbai + Delhi combined buy delivers India's premium urban audience at maximum scale.

## Planning a Mumbai Airport campaign

### Step 1: Define terminal objective
For premium, HNW and international audiences — invest in T2 international zones. For domestic business traveller reach at lower CPM — T2 domestic or T1.

### Step 2: Format mix for campaign objective

**Brand awareness**: Large-format departure hall and arrivals hall formats for maximum visual impact.

**Frequency-based recall**: Gate lounge DOOH — repeated exposure to the same audience segment across a campaign period.

**Premium positioning**: Aerobridge vinyls — highest CPM but the most powerful proximity format in the airport.

**Broad reach**: Baggage reclaim + departure hall combination — hits both arrivals and departures.

### Step 3: Duration
60-day minimum for brand awareness campaigns. Business travellers passing through Mumbai Airport 2–4 times per month will see your campaign 6–10 times in a 60-day period — building meaningful recall.

### Step 4: Creative adaptation
T2's visual environment is striking — art installations, architectural features and premium retail visual merchandising. Airport creative at Mumbai must be high-quality and visually distinctive to stand out. Minimum 2K resolution for digital screens. Premium vinyl material for all static formats.

## Mumbai Airport advertising: barter options

Mumbai Airport advertising through GMR Airports Infrastructure (which manages CSIA) is primarily cash-based. However, advertising within airport retail concessions, F&B outlets and third-party zones offers some barter flexibility for consumer goods and F&B categories.
