Choosing a media buying agency in India is one of the most consequential decisions in a brand's marketing stack. A good agency can deliver 30–50% more reach for the same budget. A mediocre one can burn through spend on the wrong formats, wrong markets and wrong timing.

This guide covers what to look for, what to ask, and how to evaluate agencies before committing your media budget.

## What does a media buying agency do?

A media buying agency handles the planning and purchasing of advertising space on behalf of a brand. This includes:

**Planning**: Identifying the right channels, formats, markets and timing for a campaign based on brand objectives, target audience and budget.

**Negotiation**: Securing advertising inventory at competitive rates through established relationships with media owners — OOH operators, metro concessionaires, radio stations, cinema chains, airport media owners.

**Execution**: Managing campaign logistics — creative specifications, installation, trafficking, proof of performance.

**Reporting**: Providing campaign analytics — reach, frequency, DEC estimates, impression delivery and post-campaign reports.

In India, media buying agencies range from large holding company agencies (GroupM, Dentsu, IPG) handling ATL campaigns for large corporates, to independent specialists focused on specific channels or campaign types (OOH, barter, transit, airport).

## The Indian media buying landscape

India's advertising market is approximately ₹1.1 lakh crore (2025 estimate), with significant differences in how media is bought:

**Television**: Dominated by large agencies with network TV relationships. Most FMCG, BFSI and auto brands use holding company agencies.

**Digital**: Performance agencies and in-house teams dominate. Independent DSPs and agency trading desks handle programmatic.

**OOH and transit**: A mix of large agency OOH divisions and independent OOH specialists. OOH is one of the few media channels where independent specialists often outperform large agency divisions.

**Barter advertising**: Specialist barter agencies like BIZEX4U handle inventory exchange campaigns. This is a niche capability that generalist agencies rarely offer effectively.

## 7 questions to ask any media buying agency

### 1. What is your rate card access?

The single most important question. Agencies negotiate with media owners regularly — those with high volume and long relationships get significantly better rates than those buying occasionally.

Ask: "What is your annual OOH spend, and which operators do you have direct relationships with?" A serious OOH agency should be able to name key relationships at Times OOH, Laqshya, Bright Outdoor, JCDecaux India and regional operators.

### 2. Do you offer barter advertising?

Most agencies don't. But if you have surplus inventory — FMCG, hospitality, consumer goods — barter can deliver 2–3x the advertising value per rupee of cost. If barter is relevant to your category, an agency without genuine barter capability is leaving money on the table.

### 3. How do you measure campaign performance?

Ask for an example post-campaign report from a past client. A good agency provides:
- Actual site photographs (proof of installation and display)
- DEC-backed reach estimates
- Frequency calculations per target market
- Comparison to plan (promised vs delivered)

Vague answers here are a red flag.

### 4. What is your fee structure?

Indian media buying agencies typically earn through:
- **Agency commission**: 10–15% of media spend, deducted from media owner invoices
- **Fixed fee**: Monthly retainer for planning and execution
- **Hybrid**: Reduced commission plus monthly fee
- **Performance bonus**: Rare but increasingly offered

Ask for a clear breakdown of how the agency earns on your account. Hidden mark-ups on production costs, printing and logistics are common in less transparent agencies.

### 5. Can you handle multi-city campaigns?

If your brand operates across India, your agency must be able to execute simultaneously in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Chennai and 30+ other cities with consistent quality. Ask for a reference from a brand that ran campaigns in 5+ cities simultaneously.

### 6. What is your creative support capability?

Many brands have master creative but need format adaptation for different media. OOH, metro, airport and transit all have specific format requirements. An agency that can't adapt creative to format specification adds friction and delay to your campaign.

### 7. How do you handle disputes with media owners?

Campaign execution problems — a site not being illuminated, a train wrap poorly applied, a radio spot not airing — happen. Ask how the agency handled a specific campaign execution problem in the past. A good answer involves proactive monitoring, immediate escalation to the media owner and either a remedy or credit.

## Red flags to avoid

**Unusually low rates quoted upfront**: Media buying isn't a commodity. Rates that seem too good usually indicate either compromised inventory or planned mark-ups elsewhere in the billing.

**No existing media owner relationships**: Agencies that claim to plan campaigns without existing relationships with major OOH operators, metro concessionaires or airport media owners will achieve worse rates and slower execution than specialists.

**No post-campaign reporting capability**: Any agency that can't show proof-of-performance photographs and deliver a post-campaign report isn't worth your budget.

**One-size-fits-all planning**: A good agency asks about your audience, distribution geography and campaign objective before recommending channels. An agency that recommends the same channel mix for every brief is not doing media planning.

**Reluctance to show client references**: Ask for 2–3 references from brands in your category or with similar campaign types. Refusal or excessive hesitation is a concern.

## Specialist vs generalist: which is better for OOH?

For OOH-heavy campaigns — outdoor, transit, metro, airport — specialist agencies consistently outperform large holding company divisions:

- **Rate access**: OOH specialists buy significantly higher volumes of specific media and get better unit rates
- **Inventory access**: Specific premium sites (top highway gantries, aerobridges, prime metro stations) are often not available through generalist agencies
- **Turnaround time**: Specialists execute faster — no internal approval layers, direct relationships with site owners
- **Barter capability**: Only specialist agencies offer genuine barter

For ATL-integrated campaigns (TV + OOH + print + digital), a large agency with OOH capabilities may be more efficient for planning coordination.

## Evaluating barter agency capability

If you're evaluating an agency for barter advertising specifically, additional criteria apply:

- **Existing barter relationships**: Ask which media owners they have active barter agreements with
- **Inventory categories handled**: Ask for examples of product categories successfully bartered
- **Documentation capability**: All barter must be properly documented with POs, commercial agreements and GST compliance. Ask for a sample barter contract structure.
- **Multi-channel barter**: The best barter agencies execute simultaneous barter across OOH, radio, cinema and transit — not just one channel
