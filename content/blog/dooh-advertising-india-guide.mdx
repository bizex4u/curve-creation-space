DOOH (Digital Out-of-Home) is India's fastest-growing advertising medium — driven by rapid urbanisation, mall expansion, metro network growth and falling screen costs. This guide covers everything brands need to know to plan and execute effective DOOH campaigns in India in 2026.

## What is DOOH advertising?

DOOH advertising uses digital screens — LED or LCD — positioned in public spaces to display advertising content. Unlike traditional OOH (printed hoardings), DOOH screens:

- Display multiple advertisers in rotation
- Support dynamic, time-based content changes
- Can be updated remotely without physical installation
- Are increasingly connected to audience measurement systems

In India, DOOH has expanded beyond traditional roadside hoardings to cover malls, airports, metro stations, fuel pumps, gyms, office lobbies, bus shelters and cinema foyers.

## The Indian DOOH landscape in 2026

India has approximately 50,000+ DOOH screens in formal networks — a number that has roughly tripled since 2019. The market is dominated by a mix of:

**National networks**: Times OOH, Clear Channel India, Laqshya Media, Kinetic India — operating large-scale networks across multiple cities and environments.

**Airport specialists**: Adsyndicate, Engiti — focused on airport terminal DOOH.

**Mall networks**: InMobi OOH, Lemma — running mall screen networks across tier 1 and tier 2 cities.

**Transit specialists**: OOH networks within metro systems and bus terminals.

## Major DOOH environments in India

### Roadside LED hoardings

The most prominent DOOH format — large LED screens on highway structures replacing or supplementing traditional static hoardings. Available across all major metros:

- **Delhi**: NH48, Ring Road, ITO flyover, South Delhi corridors
- **Mumbai**: Western Expressway, Eastern Expressway, BKC, Lower Parel
- **Bangalore**: Outer Ring Road, Old Airport Road, MG Road
- **Hyderabad**: Outer Ring Road, HITECH City corridor
- **Chennai**: OMR, Anna Salai, ECR

These screens typically run 8–12 ad slots per 90–120 second loop. Your ad plays for 10 seconds per loop — approximately 40–50 plays per screen per hour.

### Mall DOOH screens

In-mall digital screens are India's most commercially developed DOOH environment. Major mall DOOH networks cover:

- Atrium screens (large-format screens in mall central atriums)
- Corridor panels (smaller screens along walkways)
- Food court displays
- Elevator lobby screens
- Retail entrance panels

Mall DOOH reaches a captive, shopping-intent audience — peak exposure during weekend retail hours (11 AM–8 PM).

### Metro station DOOH

Delhi Metro, Mumbai Metro, Bangalore Metro and Hyderabad Metro all have digital screen networks at stations. Metro DOOH reaches daily commuters at high frequency — the same commuter sees your ad every weekday for the campaign duration.

### Airport DOOH

Airport DOOH is India's highest-CPM digital screen environment — reaching a premium, high-income audience with extended dwell time. Available at all major airports. Premium formats include:

- Mega DOOH screens in departure halls
- Digital columns and kiosks
- Baggage reclaim area screens
- Airside departure lounge screens

### Fuel station DOOH

A rapidly growing format — screens at petrol pump forecourts. Typically 2–4 minute dwell time (fuel fill duration). Networks like Hoardings India cover 2,000+ fuel station screens.

### Office and corporate lobby DOOH

Screens in office building lobbies and lifts — reaching corporate employees in a distraction-free environment. Ideal for B2B, financial services and SaaS brands.

## DOOH targeting capabilities in India

DOOH's key advantage over static OOH is targeting. Indian DOOH networks offer:

### Dayparting

Schedule your creative to specific time windows:
- Morning drive (7–10 AM) — commuters, breakfast context
- Mid-morning (10 AM–12 PM) — retail, decision-maker hours
- Afternoon (12–3 PM) — lunch, leisure, shopping
- Evening drive (5–8 PM) — commuters, F&B, entertainment
- Night (8 PM–midnight) — entertainment, OTT, food delivery

### Day of week targeting

Run campaigns only on weekdays (for corporate audiences) or weekends (for retail and leisure audiences). Particularly useful for mall and entertainment zone screens.

### Geographic targeting

Select specific screens in specific localities, malls, airports or metro stations. Build hyperlocal campaigns targeting a 2 km radius around your stores, outlets or event venues.

### Audience targeting (programmatic DOOH)

A subset of Indian DOOH networks are connected to mobile data-driven audience intelligence platforms. These allow demographic targeting based on anonymised mobile footfall data — selecting screens where your target demographic over-indexes.

## DOOH creative requirements in India

Unlike static OOH, DOOH creative must work within screen constraints:

**File format**: MP4 (video) or JPEG/PNG (static). Most networks accept both.

**Resolution**: Minimum 1920×1080 (HD) or 3840×2160 (4K for premium screens). Roadside LED screens may have custom resolutions.

**Duration**: 10 seconds per slot (standard). Some networks allow 15 or 20 second slots at premium pricing.

**File size**: Typically 50–150 MB per creative file. Networks specify maximum file size.

**Sound**: Most Indian DOOH screens do not have audio. Design for silent viewing with strong visual storytelling and minimal text.

### DOOH creative best practices

- **Lead with your brand**: You have 10 seconds. Brand must be visible in the first 2 seconds.
- **High contrast**: Screens are viewed in varying light conditions — high contrast text and imagery performs best.
- **Minimal text**: One headline, one CTA. Viewers have 5–10 seconds to absorb your message.
- **Bold visuals**: Static DOOH creative should be bold and simple. Animated content should avoid fast cuts — smooth transitions read better at screen distances.
- **QR codes**: Increasingly effective for mall and metro DOOH where viewers are stationary. Size appropriately for screen distance.

## DOOH advertising costs in India 2026

DOOH pricing varies significantly by environment, network and city. General ranges:

### Roadside LED hoardings
- Tier 2 city, secondary locations: ₹50,000–1.5 lakh per month per screen
- Tier 1 city, main corridors: ₹1.5–5 lakh per month per screen
- Premium city entry/expressway: ₹3–10 lakh per month per screen

### Mall screens
- Tier 2 mall, corridor panels: ₹30,000–80,000 per month
- Tier 1 mall, atrium screen: ₹2–8 lakh per month
- Premium mall domination package: ₹10–30 lakh per month

### Metro DOOH
- Delhi Metro digital panels: ₹50,000–2 lakh per station per month
- Network-wide metro DOOH: ₹15–40 lakh per month

### Airport DOOH
- Tier 2 airport screens: ₹50,000–2 lakh per month
- Major airport departures screens: ₹2–8 lakh per month
- Premium airport mega screens: ₹8–30 lakh per month

## Programmatic DOOH in India

Programmatic buying for DOOH is early-stage in India but growing. A small number of networks (Clear Channel India, InMobi OOH) now offer programmatic DOOH — buying screens by impression rather than by time or location block.

Benefits of programmatic DOOH:
- Impression-based buying (pay for actual audience delivery)
- Real-time campaign optimisation
- Audience data integration
- Reporting and analytics

Current limitation: Indian programmatic DOOH inventory is still limited. Most effective campaigns combine direct-bought and programmatic DOOH.

## Planning a DOOH campaign in India

### Define your environment first

Start with where your audience is: malls, metro stations, airports, roadside, office buildings? Different environments reach different audience profiles at different cost structures.

### Calculate frequency vs reach

DOOH at metro stations delivers high frequency to the same individuals. Mall DOOH delivers broader but less frequent reach. Choose based on whether your campaign goal is awareness (reach) or recall (frequency).

### Build your creative for the format

Mall screens support more detailed creative. Roadside LED is viewed at speed — maximum simplicity. Airport screens reach a captive audience — can carry more information.
